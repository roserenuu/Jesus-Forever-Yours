My Testimony

People called me a light my whole life.
I finally found the source.

A little girl from Nepal who grew up worshipping Hindu gods encountered Jesus for the first time at 24 and was never the same again.

Nepal

I grew up worshipping many gods.

I was born and raised in Kathmandu, Nepal. Every morning my mom, grandma and I would bow down to statues and chant prayers. Worship was an everyday thing. God felt like someone we had to constantly please with rituals and rules. I loved my culture and my people deeply, but I had no real connection with the faith I was raised in.

Age 8

She did not come home.

My mom was admitted to the hospital. I kept asking my dad, when is she coming back? One day I ran up the stairs and looked into his room. I saw a picture of her surrounded by flowers. I was eight years old and I had to form in my own mind that she was gone. No one told me. After her passing no one spoke her name. So I stayed silent too. And I carried the weight of losing her alone for years.

Age 14

The other side
of the world.

My dad remarried and we moved to Arizona. I adjusted to a new country, a new language, a new school, and a new mother figure all at once. At 16 I started working full time while in high school. I became my own parent. I relied entirely on myself. Life felt like one long survival mode.

Age 21

My first real prayer
was a desperate one.

A few years later I went back to Nepal and one night my sister flew off a motorbike and hit her head on the ground. She was placed in the ICU with multiple skull fractures and critical brain injuries. That was the first time I ever prayed to God. I did not know who I was praying to. I just begged him not to take her too.

One day later she was released from the ICU. Three days later she opened her eyes. A week later she was home. I knew that whoever I had prayed to had answered me.

Los Angeles

My darkest season
became my open door.

I moved to LA with no job, no car, and one suitcase. That is when I fell into depression. For the first time I could not hide from myself. Everything I had suppressed my whole life came rushing out. Grief for my mom. Trauma from the accident. Pain from letting go of nearly a decade of friendships. My soul just felt so dark.

That Christmas my boyfriend's mom gave me my very first Bible. It sat on my shelf for three months. I was too intimidated to open it. One day I finally did.

The Turning Point

"You are the light of the world, like a city on a hilltop that cannot be hidden."
Matthew 5:14

When I read that verse I immediately started crying. I felt this overwhelming presence of love and light fill my entire body. It felt like the warmest hug I had ever received. In that sacred moment my soul recognized Jesus. That is when he revealed himself to me. I was lost and I was found.

He used my pain
to heal others.

After that encounter I felt a nudge to share devotional love notes in the voice of God on social media. I didn't feel ready. I was still healing. But I had a new fire and I said yes. Messages started pouring in from people saying God was speaking to them. That became the Love Notes from God series and eventually Forever Yours.

My name is Rose. My mom's name was Renuu. Rose Renuu is my way of honoring her and carrying her with me. Today I live in freedom. No more guilt, shame, bitterness, or hiding. If he can turn my story around he can do the same for you.

Rose Renuu
